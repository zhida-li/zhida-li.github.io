Biography

Zhida Li received the B.E. and M.A.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering and Microelectronic Design from the University College Cork, Ireland, respectively. He received his Ph.D. degree in engineering science from Simon Fraser University (SFU), Canada. He was a research assistant at Tyndall National Institute, Cork, Ireland, from 2011 to 2014. He is currently a postdoctoral fellow in SFU. His current research project deals with development of machine learning algorithms for fast training and a real-time system for detecting network anomalies.

He served as Chair (2020-2021) and served as Secretary (2019-2020) of the IEEE SFU Student Branch. He serves as Secretary (2022-present) of the Membership Development Committee, IEEE Canada (R7).

He was a Technical Program Committee (TPC) Member of IEEE International Conference on High-Performance Computing and Communications (HPCC) 2020 and Session Chair at IEEE International Conference on Cyber, Physical, and Social Computing (CPSCom) 2020. Dr. Li serves as a Publicity Chair and a TPC member of CPSCom 2022.

Zhida Li was the TA for ENSC 252 (Fundamentals of Digital Logic and Design) in Fall 2015 and Summer 2016, ENSC 180 (Introduction to Engineering Analysis) in Spring 2017, ENSC 220 (Electric Circuit I) in Fall 2016, Fall 2017, Summer 2018, Summer 2020, and ENSC 427/894 (Communication Networks) in Spring 2018-2022.

Research Interests

Advisor: Prof. Ljiljana Trajkovic

  • Applications of Machine Learning Techniques for Classifying Network Anomalies
    • Process the raw network data: Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) data from RIPE and Route Views
    • Analyze and implement various machine learning algorithms as well as employing feature selection
    • Execute extensive amount of experiments on the supercomputer managed by Compute Canada
  • Development of New Machine Learning Algorithms
    • Develop two new BLS-based algorithms: (variable features BLS algorithms without (VFBLS) and with cascades (VCFBLS) for fast training
    • The developed algorithms with integrated feature selection enable utilizing a variable number of selected features
  • Development of a BGP Anomaly Detection Tool
    • Integrate various stages of the anomaly detection process
    • The tool consists of 8 modules: real-time data retrieving, feature extraction, data partition, data processing, ML algorithms, parameter selection, ML models, and classification
    • Develop a web-based application for real-time anomaly detection and off-line classification
